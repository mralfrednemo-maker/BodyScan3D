# FINAL COMPLETE MASTER DOCUMENT

## 1. Executive product definition

This product is a **self-hosted, phone-first body-part capture and reconstruction system** whose purpose is to let a non-expert operator, working in a real clinic or studio environment, capture a human body part through a short, forgiving phone workflow—preferably a handheld video sweep, or at worst a bounded set of approximately 20 photos—and obtain a **photo-like 3D asset** of that body part that is usable for three downstream value classes: photoreal viewing, stable placement of tattoos or similar 2D surface designs, and local body-shape preview such as implant-style appearance simulation. [C+G+M]

The product solves a specific, previously observed failure pattern: existing systems either demand expert-grade, delicate, hypersensitive capture discipline that breaks in real clinic or studio use, or they produce visually impressive view-only outputs that cannot carry tattoo placement or shape edits. This product must sit between those two failure modes by combining a deliberately forgiving phone capture experience, a self-hosted backend strong enough to absorb that forgiveness, and an asset representation that is simultaneously photoreal and editable. [C+G+M]

The product has three co-equal pillars: a phone capture application, which is the product’s center of gravity; a self-hosted backend, whose purpose is to make the capture side simple by doing the hard work itself; and an asset-representation strategy that simultaneously supports photoreal view synthesis and surface/shape editing. [C+G+M]

A thin web control plane may exist for job, user, and asset management. It is explicitly subordinate and must never dominate design tradeoffs. [C+G]

The governing identity, binding on all downstream subsystem prompts, is final: **this is a usable body-part capture product with a reconstruction backend, not a reconstruction demo with a capture screen.** [C+G+M]

## 2. Primary goals in priority order

1. **Intuitive, forgiving capture of a body part via phone video by a non-expert, in a real clinic or studio environment, without repeated retakes, micromanagement, or robotic precision.** This outranks raw reconstruction sophistication. [C+G+M]

2. **A photo-like 3D result from corresponding viewing angles.** The rendered result must look like the source photos or video from angles comparable to the capture trajectory, preserving believable skin tone, skin texture, and lighting plausibility. [C+G+M]

3. **Reusable asset for stable surface placement.** The asset must support stable placement of a 2D design such as a tattoo or decal across viewing angles and across re-opens of the asset. [C+G+M]

4. **Reusable asset for local body-shape preview.** The asset must support localized shape deformation that can still be rendered in a photo-like way after the edit. [C+G+M]

5. **True self-hostability.** Capture ingest, processing, storage, and serving must run on operator-controlled infrastructure, with no hidden runtime dependency on external services. [C+G+M]

6. **Predictable operational success and graceful degradation.** The product must deliver boring, repeatable success under ordinary clinic or studio conditions and degrade gracefully rather than fail hard when capture is imperfect. [C+G+M]

7. **Honest capability framing.** No implicit or casual claims of clinically reliable measurement, diagnostic accuracy, or surgically predictive certainty are allowed by default. [C+G+M]

8. **Keep the web control plane deliberately small.** It is support infrastructure, not the product value center. [C+G]

Ordering rationale: a beautiful reconstruction that requires a tense, failure-prone capture is a product failure; a merely good reconstruction from a short, forgiving capture is a product success. [C+G+M]

## 3. Non-negotiable requirements

**R1 — Self-hosted end to end.** Capture ingest, processing, storage, and serving must all run on operator-controlled infrastructure. No required third-party reconstruction API, proprietary SaaS viewer, license-server dependency, or cloud-only runtime service is allowed. Third-party libraries and model weights are acceptable only if self-hostable under a compatible license. [C+G+M]

**R2 — Phone-first capture is mandatory.** The supported core capture workflow is a consumer smartphone workflow. The product cannot depend on DSLR rigs, turntables, structured-light hardware, multi-camera rooms, or accessories to satisfy its main promise. No non-phone hardware may be required for success. [C+G+M]

**R3 — Video-first capture.** The default, advertised, and primary capture mode is a short handheld video sweep. Photo mode is fallback, not the main path. [C+G+M]

**R4 — Approximately 20-photo ceiling on the photo fallback.** If photos are used, the operator must never be asked for more than approximately twenty. Any design that quietly expands this ceiling violates the product contract. This is a product ceiling, not an algorithm preference. [C+G+M]

**R5 — Tolerant, advisory, non-gatekeeping capture guidance.** Guidance can correct, but it cannot dominate or punish. The app must not routinely abort, force restart, or reject capture because of minor motion blur, brief occlusion, mild exposure drift, small coverage gaps, or slight pacing variation. A guidance subsystem that frequently tells a real operator “try again” is a failed subsystem. [C+G+M]

**R6 — Backend absorbs complexity.** Wherever the capture side is deliberately forgiving, the backend must absorb the cost. “Make the user capture better” is not an acceptable answer to a reconstruction problem. [C+G+M]

**R7 — Photo-like output is required.** From viewpoints comparable to the capture trajectory, the primary output must look like the source imagery in casual inspection, with believable skin tone, skin texture, and lighting plausibility preserved. [C+G+M]

**R8 — Dual-capability asset representation.** The system must produce, or reliably derive from one canonical capture, both a photoreal view-synthesis-class representation and a surface-parameterized, deformation-capable representation. A pure view-only output is insufficient. Commitment is to the capability class, not to a specific algorithm. [C+G+M]

**R9 — Cross-representation consistency.** If more than one representation is used, they must be co-registered and mutually consistent. A tattoo placed through the editable representation must appear in the correct skin location when viewed through the photoreal representation, and local shape edits must not silently break correspondence. [C+G]

**R10 — No clinical or metrological claims by default.** The product shall not describe its outputs as clinically accurate measurements, diagnostic tools, or surgical predictions unless a specific configuration is formally validated to that standard. Marketing, UI copy, API naming, and default preview framing must all respect this. [C+G+M]

**R11 — Deterministic asset identity and persistence.** A given capture session must produce one canonical asset with a stable identifier. Re-opening, re-viewing, or re-deriving supported outputs must not silently mutate identity or geometry. A design placed on the skin today must land in the same skin coordinates tomorrow. [C+G+M]

**R12 — Body-part difficulty is a first-class input.** All subsystem design must assume body-part capture from the start: non-rigid tissue, weakly featured skin, specular-plus-diffuse appearance, hair and fabric edge problems, occlusion, and breathing or sway. [C+G+M]

**R13 — Privacy is a requirements-level input.** Captures are images of human bodies, often intimate areas. The stack must treat them as sensitive biometric data end to end: encrypted at rest and in transit, access-controlled, auditable, self-hosted by default, and opt-in for telemetry only. [C+G+M]

**R14 — End-to-end usability governs pass/fail.** Subsystem excellence does not rescue a product that is hard to capture with or whose output is operationally dead. Capture completion and downstream usefulness together are the governing criteria. [C+G]

## 4. Operational constraints

**Self-hosting footprint.** Target deployment is a single-tenant or operator-controlled backend, whether on-prem, private cloud, or a small managed cluster. Offline and poorly connected environments are realistic deployment contexts. No assumption of always-on external services is allowed. Infrastructure shape is deliberately not fixed at the PRD level. [C+G+M]

**Phone hardware baseline.** The capture app must run on mainstream current-generation iOS and Android smartphones. An RGB-only mainstream phone must be able to complete a usable capture. Advanced sensors may be exploited opportunistically if present but must not be required. [C+G+M]

**Active capture duration.** Normal capture is at most about 60 seconds of active shooting. Median target is at most 45 seconds on a standard body part; p95 is at most 90 seconds. Multi-minute tense capture is out of spec. [C+G+M]

**Capture environment.** Typical indoor clinic or tattoo-studio lighting is assumed: mixed warm/cool light, some reflections, and ordinary background clutter. Not a photo studio, not a lab. [C+G+M]

**Subject condition.** The subject is a living person who breathes, sways, and cannot hold a rigid pose. Non-rigid micro-motion must be absorbed by the backend, not rejected by the capture app. [C+G+M]

**Coverage imperfection.** Operators will miss coverage. The system must degrade gracefully—returning a flagged partial asset where still useful—rather than hard-failing. [C+G+M]

**Processing latency.** Order of magnitude is minutes, not hours. A scan started and left running should be ready when the operator returns to it. Exact budgets and hardware assumptions belong in later subsystem prompts. [C+G+M]

**Control-plane de-emphasis.** Admin dashboards, reporting, and web-UI polish are supporting infrastructure. They must not dominate design effort over capture and backend robustness. [C+G]

**Tolerance to imperfect capture.** The system must function under less-than-ideal capture. No requirement for near-perfect orbit smoothness, exact camera spacing, rigid pose discipline, or controlled studio behavior is acceptable just to produce an acceptable result. [C+G]

## 5. Required outputs

Per capture session, the product must produce, or be able to derive from one canonical capture:

**O1 — Photoreal viewable asset.** A representation sufficient for novel-view or viewpoint-consistent inspection that looks photo-like from near-capture viewpoints. Capability requirement, not algorithm choice. [C+G+M]

**O2 — Surface-parameterized representation.** Supports stable 2D-to-3D surface mapping, sufficient to anchor a tattoo or decal to specific skin coordinates across viewpoints and across re-opens. A UV-mapped polygonal mesh is one acceptable implementation; equivalent surface-parameterized behavior is required. [C+G+M]

**O3 — Deformable geometric proxy.** Supports local shape deformation, such as implant-like volume change, and remains photo-like after re-render. It does not need to be anatomically complete; it must be deformable and re-renderable in the regions relevant to placement and shape editing. Global watertightness is not a contract requirement. [C+G+M]

**O4 — Calibrated camera data and capture provenance.** Camera poses, intrinsics or equivalent capture-alignment data, frame selection, and intermediate artifacts needed to regenerate O1–O3 without re-capture. [C+G+M]

**O5 — Asset metadata and quality signals.** Per-asset coverage map, confidence or uncertainty summary, flagged regions, capture duration, and capture modality. [C+G+M]

**O6 — Persisted asset bundle.** Enough persistence of raw and derived data that later tattoo-placement and shape-preview workflows do not require a new capture session. [C+G+M]

**O7 — Cross-output spatial consistency guarantee.** If multiple co-registered outputs are produced, they must stay spatially consistent in geometry, surface location, and placement frame. [C+G+M]

Deliberately not required at this stage: CAD-grade meshes, STL for 3D printing, clinically validated measurements, full-body avatars, or animation-ready rigging. [C+G+M]

## 6. Acceptance criteria

All criteria are stated operationally.

### 6.1 Capture usability

A non-expert operator, after at most 10 minutes of one-time onboarding, completes a capture of a standard body part on the first attempt in at least 4 of 5 sessions. [C+G+M]

The capture flow does not abort, force restart, or block on guidance more than one corrective prompt in a typical capture, with a hard ceiling of three. [C+G+M]

Median active capture time is at most 45 seconds; p95 is at most 90 seconds; normal overall capture is at most about 60 seconds. [C+G+M]

Operators do not describe the flow as “twitchy,” “picky,” or “fragile” in structured feedback. This is binding because it is the exact failure mode the product is meant to eliminate. [C+G]

### 6.2 Reconstruction usability

At least 95% of completed captures yield a processed asset without manual parameter tuning or human intervention under normal conditions. [C+G+M]

Processing failures produce a clear diagnosis—what went wrong and what to do—rather than an opaque error. [C+G+M]

Partial or low-coverage captures produce a flagged-but-usable asset, not a hard failure, wherever physically possible. [C+G+M]

### 6.3 Photoreal appearance quality

From viewpoints within the capture trajectory, the asset passes a side-by-side naive-viewer discrimination test at near-chance rates on skin regions. This is the operational definition of “photo-like.” [C+G+M]

Skin tone, skin texture, and local lighting must be preserved well enough to avoid waxy plastic appearance, posterization, obvious smearing, floating artifacts, torn silhouettes, or severe holes. [C+G+M]

From viewpoints moderately outside the capture trajectory, soft blur or slight ghosting is acceptable; color inversion, floating blobs, torn silhouettes, or obvious holes are not. [C+G+M]

### 6.4 Tattoo-placement suitability

A 2D design placed through the surface-placement foundation remains visually attached to the same skin location across the full supported viewing range. No swimming, floating, sliding, or detachment. [C+G+M]

Placement persists across save/reload and is reproducible: same design plus same parameters means same skin coordinates every time. [C+G+M]

The workflow foundation supports at minimum position, orientation, scale, and conformance to local skin curvature. [C+G+M]

The underlying representation provides contiguous, usable surface parameterization over placement-relevant regions, not merely a viewer-level effect. [C+G]

### 6.5 Surgical-preview suitability

A local shape edit on the deformable proxy produces a post-edit render that remains photo-like, with skin-texture and lighting continuity preserved across the edited region. [C+G+M]

The edit is local, reversible, and parameterized. Unaffected regions remain unaffected. [C+G+M]

The preview is never framed as a medical prediction. UI and API enforce preview framing by construction. [C+G+M]

### 6.6 System robustness

The end-to-end pipeline—capture to upload to processing to usable asset—completes in at least 95% of attempts under normal conditions on supported devices. [C+G+M]

No single transient failure, such as network interruption, app backgrounding, or backend worker restart, loses raw capture data. [C+G]

The backend runs on operator-controlled infrastructure with documented installation, upgrade, and backup procedures. [C+G]

Graceful degradation under sub-optimal input, such as lower-resolution texture or flagged low-confidence regions, is required instead of hard failure. [C+G+M]

## 7. Failure conditions

Any of the following, if present in the shipped product, is a product failure, not a minor defect.

**F1 — Gatekeeping capture.** The capture app frequently refuses to proceed, interrupts the operator, or insists on retakes due to its own quality-gate sensitivity. Highest-priority failure to avoid. [C+G+M]

**F2 — Photo-mode creep.** Practical use needs more than about 20 photos, or operators are pushed toward photo mode because video mode is fragile. [C+G+M]

**F3 — Fragile video pipeline.** Normal handheld video sweeps on mainstream phones reconstruct unreliably or require unusually steady, slow, expert motion. [C+G+M]

**F4 — Demo-quality, workflow-useless outputs.** The asset looks impressive when orbited but cannot support stable surface placement, cannot be deformed, or cannot be re-opened with the same geometry. A splat-only or view-only output falls here. [C+G+M]

**F5 — Self-hosting in name only.** The stack technically runs on operator infrastructure but depends at runtime on an external API, proprietary viewer, license server, or other hidden runtime dependency. [C+G+M]

**F6 — Silent overclaiming.** UI, API, or marketing frames outputs as clinically accurate, diagnostic, or metrologically trustworthy without validation. [C+G+M]

**F7 — Capture-side shifting of backend responsibility.** The product is made to work mainly by demanding more careful capture from the operator instead of strengthening the backend. Direct violation of R6. [C+G+M]

**F8 — Asset identity drift.** The same capture, re-processed or re-opened, silently changes geometry or placement correspondence. A tattoo placed yesterday landing elsewhere today is product failure. [C+G+M]

**F9 — Hidden expertise gate.** Acceptable results depend on techniques a non-expert operator would not naturally perform, even when demos look good. [C+G+M]

**F10 — Control-plane drift.** Design effort shifts toward web-UI polish while mobile capture and backend remain fragile. [C+G+M]

**F11 — Ideal-conditions success only.** The product works only under narrow lighting, subject, motion, or operator conditions. Failure even when the narrow-condition demo is impressive. [C+G]

## 8. Out of scope

Explicitly out of scope at this stage, and not allowed to drive architectural decisions:

* Full-body avatars, rigging, or animation. [C+G+M]
* Real-time on-device 3D reconstruction beyond lightweight guidance feedback. [C+G+M]
* Clinical certification, regulatory approval pathways, or measurement-grade accuracy claims. [C+G+M]
* Multi-user collaborative editing, marketplaces, or sharing/social features. [C+G+M]
* Non-human subjects and arbitrary-object scanning outside the body-part focus. [C+G+M]
* Rich web control plane beyond basic capture, job, user, and asset management. [C+G+M]
* Hardware accessories such as rigs, lights, markers, or external cameras. [C+G+M]
* Development of the actual tattoo-placement UI and surgical-simulation UI as products. This phase delivers asset-level foundations. [C+G+M]
* Advanced tattoo behaviors such as ink diffusion, healing, or aging. [C+G+M]
* Biomechanical or physics-based soft-tissue simulation. [C+G+M]
* Final architecture selection, detailed subsystem design, UI copy, implementation roadmap, or commercial packaging decisions. [C+G+M]
* Product naming and branding. [C+G]

## 9. Critical risks and assumptions

These are assumptions later prompts must test, not accept.

**A1 — One capture may need multiple aligned representations.** It remains open whether one underlying representation can serve photoreal viewing, stable surface placement, and local deformation, or whether a small set of co-registered representations is required. [C+G+M]

**A2 — View-synthesis and edit-ready geometry may be technically divergent.** State-of-the-art photoreal view synthesis and clean deformation-ready structure may require different pipelines, increasing compute cost and latency. [C+G+M]

**A3 — Handheld RGB video may or may not contain enough signal.** A short mainstream-phone RGB sweep is assumed to provide enough parallax and texture variance for robust body-part reconstruction, but this must be validated empirically. [C+G+M]

**A4 — Skin is a difficult signal source.** It is weakly featured in many regions, sometimes reflective, and often non-rigid. Feature-based or geometry-extraction subsystems must account for this directly. [C+G+M]

**A5 — Non-rigid human micro-motion is tractable.** Breathing and sway are assumed to be absorbable by the backend without tracking collapse. This is load-bearing. [C+G+M]

**A6 — Stable tattoo placement likely requires real surface parameterization.** Viewer realism alone is unlikely to be enough. A surface-anchoring representation should be treated as the default assumption unless disproven. [C+G+M]

**A7 — Surgical-style preview may be achievable with local geometric-proxy deformation plus photoreal re-render.** Full biomechanics is assumed not to be required for the target preview use, but that sufficiency still has to be validated. [C+G+M]

**A8 — “Photo-like” is the correct quality bar, not “metrologically accurate.”** The product intentionally trades measurement claims for visual plausibility and capture ease. Any drift back toward accuracy claims changes the product category. [C+G+M]

**A9 — Self-hosted processing may challenge latency budgets.** It remains unresolved whether the required backend can deliver minutes-not-hours turnaround on realistic operator-controlled hardware. [C+G+M]

**A10 — Forgiving capture and reliable reconstruction are in real tension.** Guidance must stay helpful without becoming blocking, but backend success still requires sufficient signal. This is the central design tension of the capture app. [C+G+M]

**A11 — The defensible product value lies in the capture-to-usable-asset pipeline, not the reconstruction engine alone.** Later work must preserve that framing rather than commoditizing the capture layer to showcase backend tech. [C+G+M]

**A12 — Privacy is architectural, not a bolt-on.** Storage, access control, provenance retention, and deployment choices are all shaped by the sensitivity of body-part imagery. [C+G+M]

**A13 — The approximately 20-photo ceiling may be too tight for some body parts.** The ceiling is a product constraint; later prompts must test where it remains feasible and where a stricter video-first stance is better than widening the photo budget. [C+G+M]

## 10. Final product contract summary

This product is a **self-hosted, phone-first body-part capture system** that turns a short, forgiving handheld video—or, as bounded fallback, no more than about 20 photos—into a **photo-like 3D asset** that supports photoreal viewing, stable tattoo or surface placement, and local body-shape preview. [C+G+M]

Binding clauses on all downstream subsystem prompts:

* The capture application is the product’s center of gravity: intuitive, video-first, non-gatekeeping. The previous failure mode was hypersensitive capture, and it must not recur. [C+G+M]
* The backend exists to absorb the cost of that forgiveness. It must turn imperfect but sufficient capture into outputs that are both photo-like and reusable. A view-only output does not satisfy the contract. [C+G+M]
* The system is truly self-hosted end to end, preserves provenance and asset identity, treats sensitive body imagery as first-class private data, and makes no default clinical or metrological claims. [C+G+M]
* The web control plane is intentionally small. [C+G+M]
* End-to-end usability—capture completion together with downstream usefulness—is the governing pass/fail criterion. [C+G]
* The product’s identity is a capture-to-usable-asset pipeline for human body parts, not a reconstruction benchmark with a phone front-end. [C+G+M]

Any later subsystem design that pushes complexity back onto the operator, relies on runtime cloud services, produces workflow-dead outputs, or overclaims accuracy is rejected at the requirements level. [C+G+M]

Outside the supported viewing envelope, mild blur or ghosting is acceptable; tears, inversions, floaters, and obvious holes are not. Structural collapse is not acceptable. [P+G+C][PRD]
It must be reproducible under a fixed asset and fixed derivation context: same canonical asset, same compatible pipeline version, same viewpoint, same practical output, modulo only documented low-level nondeterminism that does not change effective behavior. Hidden randomness that changes practical results is unacceptable. [P+G+C][PRD]
R-OUT-2 — Surface-anchored placement
The placement foundation must provide contiguous, usable, reproducible surface parameterization over every placement-relevant region. No zipper tears, no degenerate slivers crossing the placement area, and no avoidable seams routed through intended placement zones. Patchwise or region-scoped parameterization is acceptable where honest and flagged; fake continuity is not. [P+G+C][PRD]
“Stable placement” means a design with parameter tuple P remains visually attached to the same skin location across the supported viewing range with no swimming, sliding, floating, or detachment. “Reproducible placement” means (asset id, derivation context, design, P) → same skin coordinates across save/reload, reopen, compatible re-derivation, and replay. [P+G+C][PRD]
At minimum this output must support position, orientation, scale, and conformance to local skin curvature. Viewer-only projection effects without real surface parameterization do not satisfy the contract. A viewer trick that only appears attached from one rendering path does not qualify. [P+G+C][PRD]
A UV-mapped polygonal mesh is one acceptable implementation; any representation exposing equivalent surface-parameterized behavior with the same stability, persistence, and reproducibility guarantees also satisfies the contract. [P+G+C][PRD]
R-OUT-3 — Deformable geometric proxy
The deformable proxy must expose local, parameterized, reversible, non-destructive shape edits in placement-relevant regions. It does not mean arbitrary global remeshing or full biomechanical simulation. [P+G+C][PRD]
Edits must be bounded: regions outside the declared edit support must not drift unintentionally. After edit, the photoreal re-render must remain photo-like in the edited region, preserving skin-texture continuity, lighting plausibility, and silhouette integrity across the edit boundary. A geometric edit that breaks appearance quality fails this output. [P+G+C][PRD]
Edits must be replayable: same edit parameters on the same asset under the same derivation context yield the same geometry and the same rendered behavior. Edits must not break R-OUT-2 correspondence in unedited regions, and may degrade R-OUT-2 in edited regions only in a well-defined, documented way. This output is an appearance-preview substrate, not a medical simulator, and the product must not imply otherwise. [P+G+C][PRD]
R-OUT-4 — Provenance and regeneration
The provenance bundle must be sufficient to regenerate all derived representations offline, on operator-controlled infrastructure, without the subject being present again. [P+G+C][PRD]
It must be pipeline-version-stamped so reproducibility is defined per version and upgrades are auditable. Deterministic re-derivation is required: regenerated outputs must remain bound to the same asset identity and the same correspondence frame. Close but slightly shifted is failure, not tolerance. [P+G+C][PRD]
Changing the reconstruction algorithm or pipeline version must produce a new derivation under a new versioned context, never a silent mutation of an existing usable asset. [P+G+C][PRD]
R-OUT-5 — Metadata, confidence, and flagged-region outputs
This is the product’s honesty layer. It must explicitly describe where coverage is partial, where confidence is low, and where downstream workflows should warn or refuse instead of acting as though geometry trust is high. [P+G+C][PRD]
Coverage and confidence must be machine-readable, not merely descriptive text for a human. Downstream placement and preview tooling must be able to detect where the asset is usable and where it is flagged. [P+G+C][PRD]
Flagged regions must be first-class and propagate across R-OUT-1, R-OUT-2, and R-OUT-3 so all three agree on where the asset should not be strongly trusted. Pipeline fingerprint must be part of the asset, not merely an external log entry. [P+G+C][PRD]
R-OUT-6 — Persisted canonical asset bundle
The persisted asset must have a single canonical asset ID stable for the lifetime of the asset. It must be durable enough that the operator can save, reopen, archive, move, and later reuse the asset for placement and preview workflows without recapture. [P+G+C][PRD]
Atomic write/read semantics are required: partial writes must not produce partially valid assets. The bundle must be self-contained enough that a valid asset can be archived and reopened on operator infrastructure without external runtime dependencies. [P+G+C][PRD]
R-OUT-7 — Cross-output spatial consistency
A single shared canonical frame of reference is binding; all three geometric/appearance representations are expressed in or registered to it. The same skin point must resolve to the same skin point across photoreal viewing, placement, and deformation contexts. [P+G+C][PRD]
At the contract level, drift between representations must be visually undetectable in placement and deformation workflows. Exact numeric drift budgets belong in later prompts. Correspondence must survive save/reload, reopen, and compatible re-derivation, not merely hold inside a single viewer session. [P+G+C][PRD]
What must remain consistent across the outputs is explicit: canonical asset identity, canonical coordinate frame, body-part pose frame, skin-point correspondence, placement anchors, edit-region location, and flagged-region semantics. [P+G+C][PRD]
4. Representation strategy decision
The binding decision is: multiple co-registered representations derived from a single canonical capture. [P+G+C][PRD]
The reason is contractual, not stylistic. The three value classes impose incompatible demands. Photoreal view synthesis favors implicit, appearance-rich rendering behavior. Stable tattoo placement requires explicit, contiguous, reproducible surface parameterization. Local shape preview requires an explicit geometric substrate supporting bounded, reversible, re-renderable edits. No peer document presented a credible basis for assuming one representation can satisfy all three at the required quality and persistence bar by default. [P+G+C][PRD]
The closure must be stated precisely:
The full output capability set is closed at five classes: photoreal viewing, surface-anchored placement, deformable preview, provenance/regeneration, and metadata/confidence signaling. Downstream prompts may not expand this set. [P+G+C][PRD]
Within that set, exactly three classes are co-registered geometric/appearance representations in the canonical frame: photoreal view, surface-anchored placement, and deformable proxy. [P+G+C][PRD]
Later prompts may implement multiple capability classes on one physical data structure only if every capability guarantee is demonstrably satisfied without compromise. They may not invent per-use-case side representations to paper over core gaps. [P+G+C][PRD]
Responsibilities are separated as follows:
Photoreal view owns supported-view appearance quality and novel-view behavior near the capture path. It does not own placement authority or editability. [P+G+C]
Surface-anchored placement owns stable 2D↔3D anchoring and repeatable placement behavior. It does not own appearance. [P+G+C]
Deformable proxy owns bounded geometric edits and their replayable semantics. It does not own unedited-region viewing quality, but it must not contradict the photoreal view when edited. [P+G+C]
Provenance bundle owns derivation, regeneration, and auditability. [P+G+C]
Metadata/confidence layer owns trust signaling and flagged-region semantics. [P+G+C]
The canonical capture plus provenance bundle is the source of truth. Representations are derivations. Drift between them is a product failure, not a tuning issue. [P+G+C][PRD]
5. Mesh requirement decision
A surface-based editable representation is mandatory. A pure photoreal neural, splat, or other view-only representation is insufficient because it cannot by itself guarantee stable surface parameterization, repeatable placement tuples, and bounded reversible local shape edits. [P+G+C][PRD]
A UV-mapped polygonal mesh is one explicitly acceptable implementation of R-OUT-2 and a common substrate for R-OUT-3. Later prompts may adopt it without further contractual justification. [P+G+C][PRD]
Equivalent surface-parameterized behavior also satisfies the contract, but only if it demonstrably provides all of the following: contiguous and reproducible surface parameterization over placement-relevant regions; stable and repeatable anchoring; local parameterized deformation; persistence across save/reload and re-derivation; and co-registration with the photoreal output without unacceptable drift. A clever viewer-layer approximation that behaves like a surface only temporarily does not qualify. [P+G+C][PRD]
Global watertightness and anatomical completeness are not mandatory. Patchwise or region-scoped parameterization over placement-relevant regions is acceptable. Unusable regions must be flagged through R-OUT-5, not papered over. What is mandatory is usable local continuity and parameterization in the regions the product claims are valid for placement and preview. [P+G+C][PRD]
6. Persistence and identity guarantees
These guarantees apply to the canonical asset and every required derived output. [P+G+C][PRD]
Stable identifier. One capture session yields one canonical asset ID, immutable for the life of the asset. Later compatible derivations belong to that asset identity; they do not erase it. [P+G+C][PRD]
Save/reopen invariance. Reopening an asset must preserve the same asset identity, the same canonical coordinate frame, the same placement frame, and the same interpretation of stored edit and placement parameters. Reopen must not silently mutate geometry semantics. [P+G+C][PRD]
Re-render reproducibility. Same asset, same compatible derivation context, same viewpoint, same edit/placement parameters, same effective output behavior, modulo only documented low-level nondeterminism. Hidden randomness that changes practical behavior is unacceptable. [P+G+C][PRD]
Re-derivation stability. The canonical capture plus the provenance bundle must deterministically reproduce every required derived representation without recapture. A pipeline-version change must be explicit and auditable; it must never silently overwrite an earlier derivation while pretending nothing changed. Close but slightly shifted is failure, not tolerance. [P+G+C][PRD]
Placement reproducibility. (asset id, pipeline version, design, placement parameters) → same skin coordinates. Placing the same tattoo twice with the same parameters must land on the same skin. Yesterday’s placement is still yesterday’s placement tomorrow. [P+G+C][PRD]
Preview-workflow replay. Reopening a stored local shape edit must restore the same editable state and produce the same behavior, not a newly interpreted approximation. Preview edits are persisted state, not ephemeral viewer tricks. [P+G+C][PRD]
Upgrade policy. Pipeline-version change is an explicit, operator-visible event producing a new derivation. Silent migration of assets to a new reconstruction under the old derivation state is forbidden. [P+G+C][PRD]
Cross-representation identity. The same point of skin is the same point of skin across all three representations for an asset under a fixed derivation. [P+G+C][PRD]

Photoreal preview
Required: photo-like appearance from within-trajectory viewpoints to the PRD bar, with preserved skin tone, texture, and lighting; graceful blur or mild ghosting outside the trajectory; no tears, inversions, floaters, or obvious holes. [P+G+C][PRD]

Tattoo placement
Required: visual attachment to the same skin location across the supported viewing range; reproducibility across save/reload and compatible re-derivation; support for position, orientation, scale, and curvature conformance; contiguous usable parameterization in placement-relevant regions. [P+G+C][PRD]
Not required: global watertightness, whole-body UV continuity, or print-ready topology. [P+G+C][PRD]
Local shape preview
Required: bounded, reversible, parameterized local edits that re-render photo-like with continuous skin texture and lighting across the edit boundary while unaffected regions remain visually and geometrically unaffected. [P+G+C][PRD]
Not required: biomechanics-grade soft-tissue simulation, diagnostic prediction, or surgically predictive accuracy. [P+G+C][PRD]
Explicitly not required at this stage
Global watertightness; CAD-grade geometry or tolerancing; metrological, diagnostic, or surgically predictive accuracy; full-body avatars or anatomical completeness beyond the captured body part; print-ready meshes; rigging, skinning, or animation; real-time on-device reconstruction beyond lightweight guidance; editing UX for tattoo placement or surgical preview; and physics-grade soft-tissue simulation. 8. Output-level failure conditions
Any of the following is a contract-level failure regardless of how impressive the reconstruction looks. [P+G+C][PRD]
OF-1 — Impressive-orbit, dead-asset. The photoreal result looks beautiful, but the asset cannot support stable placement, cannot be deformed, or cannot be reopened with the same usable state. This is the highest-priority output failure. [P+G+C][PRD]
OF-2 — One-shot editability. The asset can be edited or placed in-session once but cannot be persistently reopened with the same edit or placement intact. [P+G+C][PRD]
OF-3 — Deformation breaks realism. Local shape edits produce waxy smearing, torn texture, silhouette breaks, or lighting collapse across the edit boundary. [P+G+C][PRD]

Capture philosophy and operator model
The app is a tolerant advisor, with a narrow set of hard stops. It is not a strict director, not a silent recorder, and not a generic camera-with-overlay. The “hybrid” label is rejected because it invites later drift toward director behavior under engineering pressure. [P+G+C][PRD]
“Forgiving” means, operationally, that the default behavior of the app during capture is silence; silence is the correct state and any nudge is an exception. The app quietly corrects through internal frame weighting, silent exposure hinting, silent tracking recovery, and silent discarding of useless frames. Real-time guidance appears only at the two endpoints of the envelope: very gentle passive signals that the capture is going well, and a minimum of hard stops when it is objectively broken. Everything in between is absorbed without comment. [P+G+C][PRD]
“Non-gatekeeping guidance” means the app cannot prevent capture from continuing on the basis of its own quality judgment. It can suggest, reassure, and flag after the fact, but it cannot block. A partial, messy, or imperfect capture proceeds to upload unless it is genuinely below the processable floor or the operator cancels. [P+G+C][PRD][OC]
The operator model is a competent adult in a working clinic or studio environment, moving naturally, not rehearsing a robotic orbit. The app is their competent colleague, not their instructor. No scanning skill is assumed. Any later UX iteration that slides toward director behavior violates the product constraints. [P+G+C][PRD]
Capture modes and their roles
The product has two operator-visible main modes and one bounded recovery action. [P+G+C][PRD]
M1 — Video sweep (default, primary). A short handheld sweep, roughly 30–60 seconds of active shooting, with the operator moving around the body part in an approximate arc. This is the advertised mode, the one onboarding teaches, and the one all product quality claims assume. [P+G+C][PRD]
M2 — Assisted auto-photo fallback (bounded, operator-visible). A structured sequence capped at approximately 20 photos. The app guides the operator toward viewpoints and captures frames automatically when stability, framing, and exposure thresholds are met. The operator’s physical action is still “move around the body part”; the app does the shutter. The ~20-photo ceiling is a hard product rule. End-to-end operator flow
Step 1 — Start session. The operator opens the app, picks or confirms the subject record, and selects the body part from a compact template list. “Other” is always accepted; no template is mandatory. [P+G+C][PRD]
Step 2 — Live preview and body-part targeting. The camera opens into a minimal preview with a soft framing hint appropriate to the template. The operator identifies the target body part with a rough selection gesture — a tap, a loose outline, or a rough brush region. The app refines this into a working target halo. Precision is not demanded; targeting is for capture guidance alignment only, not final geometry. If refinement is visibly wrong, the operator adjusts once and continues. No calibration ritual. [P+G+C][PRD]
Step 3 — Pre-capture posture. Minimal prep guidance only: expose the region naturally, keep the body part visible, and plan one short sweep. No lengthy checklist or expert instructions. A single Start button. [P+G+C][PRD]
Step 4 — Active capture. The operator taps Start and performs a natural arc sweep. The screen shows exactly four things: the live camera view, the soft target halo, a faint non-numeric coverage hint that fills in as motion accumulates, and a small recording indicator. [P+G+C][PRD]

Usable with flagged regions: processing now; a few regions will come back flagged in the asset.
Worth a short patch: processable, but a ~5–10 second patch would noticeably improve a specific region; the operator may approve or skip.
Retry recommended: below the processable floor; a short fresh attempt is advised, never as operator blame.

Prompt-2-outcome
Shot-taking and “good enough” logic
The system optimizes for acceptable coverage, not perfect coverage. Perfect coverage is not a product goal and cannot be enforced. Video is treated as continuous oversampling of the view sphere. The backend is the curator; the operator never picks frames. The operator moves naturally around the subject; the system finds the useful frames inside that footage. [P+G+C][PRD]
“Enough useful signal” — the processable floor — is defined by four metrics: coverage breadth over the placement-relevant region; view diversity and parallax sufficient to support reconstruction; target persistence, meaning the body part remained in frame for a meaningful fraction of the sweep; and minimum stable frame count the backend can confidently curate from. The operator is never shown these internals as scores or percentages. [P+G+C][PRD][OC]
What the capture system silently tolerates: mild motion blur on individual frames, brief occlusions, exposure drift and mixed lighting, small coverage gaps, minor pacing variation, handheld shake, breathing, sway, small pose drift, brief tracking dropouts, momentary target-edge crop, and slight path wobble. [P+G+C][PRD]
What justifies an in-session intervention: camera not pointed at the subject for a sustained window, scene too dark to extract any signal, tracking collapsed and unrecovered, subject out of frame for a sustained window, or a session running well past normal duration with apparent operator unawareness. What justifies a post-debounce warning: one major side of the body part never observed, or persistent blur across a meaningful stretch. This appears as a single soft nudge, never as repeated micro-corrections. Any policy that gates capture on sub-second per-frame quality metrics is explicitly rejected; those belong to the backend. [P+G+C][PRD]

Guidance is primarily visual and minimal. Subtle haptic feedback is reserved for hard-stop events only, not for general state changes. There is no audio guidance and no voice prompts — they would make the app feel intrusive, judgmental, and overly active. [P+G+C][PRD]

Hard stops function as a calm modal offering to resume or end, not a punitive rejection. Maximum acceptable correction frequency is median zero nudges per capture, p95 no more than one gentle nudge, and a hard ceiling of three total interventions in a session before it is considered a UX regression. Video-to-frame reduction strategy
The M1 strategy is hybrid: novelty-first, quality-weighted, redundancy-suppressed, and coverage-floor-enforced. Uniform sampling is too blunt. Quality-only selection recreates the very failure mode the product was designed to avoid. Novelty-only selection can keep the wrong representative inside a local neighborhood. The binding principle is contribution-weighted tolerance, not average-image-quality fetishism.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Binding principles
Unique moderate-quality evidence outranks pristine duplicates of already strong views.
Duplicate excellence does not cancel missing-sector weakness.
No single minor metric may veto a frame that adds unique coverage.
Banded hysteresis, not knife-edge, applies across selection and verdict boundaries.

Reduction pipeline
Step 1 — Candidate pass. Decode candidates from raw segments at roughly 5–10 candidates per second of active M1, denser near keyframe hints, motion minima, novelty spikes, and the first stable observations after interruptions. Candidate rate is not final keep rate.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Step 2 — Garbage-floor exclusion only. Discard only structurally unusable candidates: total black interruption-edge frames, parse failures, fully covered/saturated frames, or frames wholly off-target for sustained windows confirmed by metadata. Ordinary blur, wobble, brief crop, and short tracking-loss windows are scored and deprioritized, not hard-dropped.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Step 3 — Novelty graph with rank-ordered fallback. Compute pairwise novelty in priority order: AR-pose delta, then IMU/orientation delta, then lightweight visual-descriptor distance. The regime used must be logged in provenance.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Step 4 — Quality annotation via utility vector. Compute bounded sharpness, motion damage, target persistence, exposure/readability confidence, temporal support, and prior-quality availability. These axes help pick the best representative within a local neighborhood; they do not veto unique coverage.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Step 5 — Cluster-based redundancy suppression. Admit candidates that add non-trivial novelty or close a sparse coverage region and are not strictly dominated by an already-admitted neighbor in both novelty and quality. Use a local redundancy window of about 0.25–0.5 seconds adapted by IMU-estimated displacement rather than a fixed frame count. Protect the first stable observations after an interruption because they often re-establish missing sectors.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Step 6 — Coverage-floor enforcement. After first-pass suppression, inspect directional-bin occupancy and atlas-cell strength. For any occupied bin below its minimum support floor, re-admit the best available candidate even if only moderate quality. This is a binding anti-perfectionism guardrail.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Step 7 — Reserve-tier materialization. All candidates that lost local suppression contests but are not garbage enter the extended candidate tier with full utility vectors and reason codes. Reconstruction may escalate into reserve without re-running curation.

"Good enough to process" decision logic
The verdict order is strict: PROCESS_CLEAN > PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS > PATCH_RECOMMENDED > RETRY_RECOMMENDED, with ties breaking toward processing and hysteresis favoring the less disruptive outcome near boundaries.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
PROCESS_CLEAN
A session is PROCESS_CLEAN when it clears a healthy operating band: on standard M1, typically in the 80–150 primary observation band with broad directional coverage and no dominant weak bin; on M2, a strong portion of the bounded set remains distinct and useful. Parallax is sufficient, no severe event dominates, and the metadata tier is coherent enough for later pose/reconstruction to proceed without obvious rescue. These are operating targets, not universal gates.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS
A session is PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS when it is above the true processable floor but one or more weak regions remain: thin but real parallax, one under-observed side, one weak atlas cluster, one non-severe segment failure, or low-but-coherent metadata tier. This should be common and is a success state, not a soft failure. Weaknesses propagate downstream honestly.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
PATCH_RECOMMENDED
A session is PATCH_RECOMMENDED only when all PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS conditions are met, weakness is localized and concretely describable in halo+gravity-anchored terms, a bounded patch is plausibly sufficient to move the result materially toward PROCESS_CLEAN, and both patch counters remain inside bounds: less than 1 patch for the current review cycle and less than 2 over the session lifetime. Patch is always skippable.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
RETRY_RECOMMENDED
A session is RETRY_RECOMMENDED only for foundational failure: global darkness, target mostly absent, catastrophic frame starvation after curation, one-frame-class capture with zero novelty, metadata collapse, or diffuse weakness that no bounded patch could honestly close. Retry is never for ordinary handheld messiness.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Floor governance
The processable floor must be explicit, auditable, and versioned with the FSCQI pipeline fingerprint. It may evolve only through a new version, never through silent tuning drift. That is the safeguard against a curator becoming a hidden perfectionist gate.

Coverage and weak-region detection
Coverage is modeled at two levels and both are guidance-class only. The first is global provisional view sectors: roughly 12–24 coarse target-facing sectors derived from pose priors when available or from gravity/orientation/motion clustering when not. The second is a normalized target atlas: overlapping halo-anchored cells that track usable-observation count, best observation quality, angular diversity, edge-touch rate, occlusion risk, and marginal-evidence dependence.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Every weak-region output must carry the explicit field coverage_semantics: "apparent_view_direction_relative_to_halo_anchor" so later prompts do not over-read it as solved 3D geometry. These are capture-derived priors, not final surface truth.

Weak-region classification remains deliberately three-tiered:
Seen once badly: one marginal observation only.
Seen many times weakly: repeated evidence but low diversity or repeated degradation.
Not really seen: effectively no reliable evidence.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
This split matters because sparse marginal evidence, repeated weak evidence, and genuine absence drive different patch-vs-flag-vs-retry behavior. A binary weak/unusable split loses that nuance.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Patch target hints come from the overlap of weak provisional sectors, weak atlas clusters, and patchability scoring. They must be operator-legible in halo+gravity-relative language and must never drift into final-geometry wording.

Patch recommendation logic
Patch is bounded, optional, conservative, append-only, and never a stealth second session. One patch round per review cycle and a defensive session-level soft cap of 2 are binding. Video patches are bounded to about 10 seconds and photo patches to about 5 photos.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
A patch is justified only when all three conditions hold: the session is already processable; the weakness is localized rather than diffuse; and a bounded patch is plausibly sufficient to move the result materially toward clean processing. Patch is not for vague “more data might help” optimism.
ein_prompt_final_tnfk3mmw
Retry, not patch, is justified when failure is foundational: mostly off-target, scene too dark, one-view-only evidence, diffuse missingness, wrong body part, catastrophic corruption, or metadata collapse that a patch would inherit. Patch must never substitute for retry below the true floor.

All are signed by dense_pipeline_fingerprint and bound to canonical session identity plus pose_version, isolation_version, and geometry_version. [P+G+C][OC]
Reconstruction strategy
DG is a component-local-first, hybrid, staged, uncertainty-aware dense reconstruction layer. It is explicitly not a single global-fusion solve, not model-locked, and not allowed to force session-global merging. REG component structure is authoritative. Session-level harmonization only aligns outputs into the shared body_part_pose_frame; cosmetic single-component collapse is forbidden. [P+G+C][REG]
Stage D1 — Dense regime classification. Each session and each component are classified by modality mix, component topology, camera-state stability, metadata tier, scale regime, weak-region distribution, patch lineage, and observation-state mix. This determines whether a component is eligible for full dense fusion, conservative fragment extraction only, or support-only output. Silent regime drift is forbidden and the regime decision is logged. [P+G+C][REG][FSCQI]
Stage D2 — Per-observation dense-support estimation. For admitted observations, DG computes dense support in a view-centric form first: depth-like hypotheses, multi-view consistency scores, visibility support, photometric support summaries, and boundary reliability. Support is restricted to target-matted regions per target_alpha_soft, target_core_mask, and exclusion_mask, with target_adjacent_soft_context_band admitted only into context_geometry under bounded rescue. The stage is capability-defined, not library-defined. [P+G+C][SIAT][REG]
Stage D3 — Per-component coupled-trio fusion. DG fuses view-centric support into a component-local dense representation composed of fused oriented support points, a conservative volumetric or implicit support field for topology reasoning, and visibility / support-count accumulators for later appearance and QC. This coupled-trio posture is stronger than jumping directly to a single mesh because support can remain strong where surface extraction must stay weak. Fusion honors pose_covariance_digest, feature_support_regime, boundary_confidence_channel, ambiguity tags, and utility vectors as weighting fields. Soft alpha weighting is preserved end-to-end; binarization at fusion time is forbidden. [P+G+C][SIAT][REG][FSCQI]
Stage D4 — Conservative surface extraction with thickness-aware handling. DG extracts partial surfaces from fused support only where support count, support confidence, support regime, pose confidence, boundary confidence, and cross-view consistency jointly cross a calibrated admissibility floor. The default export posture is conservative fragments and open boundaries, not forced closure. Local regularization is admissible for numerical stability; exported geometry may not claim more certainty than the support field earns. Thickness-aware fusion is binding for thin anatomical structures such as ear flaps, skin folds, nostril rims, and fingertip margins: front-and-back surfaces may not collapse into one plane and support volumes may not balloon unnaturally. Where thickness cannot be resolved honestly, the region is downgraded to weak_surface_fragment or skeletal_support_only with weakness_reason=poor_dense_support. [P+G+C][OC]
Stage D5 — Confidence, topology, weakness, and usefulness derivation. DG derives surface_confidence_field, topology_confidence_class, hole_and_open_boundary_map, weak_geometry_zones with weakness_reason, usefulness_zones, appearance_risk_flags, geometry_ready_for_* summaries, measurement_use_prohibited, and severe concern flags. [P+G+C][OC]
Stage D6 — Bounded fragment-relation analysis. DG examines REG component_relations together with fragment proximity, body_part_pose_frame alignment, shared target identity, scale compatibility, and local dense corroboration, and emits fragment_relations as no_merge_claim, weak_bridge, or strong_bridge. strong_bridge is permitted only when REG supports a bridge, scale regime is compatible, camera-state conditions do not force incompatibility, and dense support corroborates the bridge with continuous local evidence. Otherwise fragment_relations remains no_merge_claim or weak_bridge. [P+G+C][REG]
Stage D7 — Patch integration. Patch observations are integrated under append-only geometry_version lineage according to the patch policy below. Local update is preferred. Session-global re-fusion is admissible only when REG itself emitted a compatible broader pose_version re-opt and all compatibility conditions hold. Silent session-global rewrite is forbidden. [P+G+C][REG][MOB]
Stage D8 — Provenance emission. Named artifacts, decision logs, admission logs, and lineage bindings are emitted and signed. Deterministic practical behavior at fixed dense_pipeline_fingerprint is required modulo documented low-level nondeterminism that does not change practical downstream behavior. [P+C][OC]
The internally-closed-for-computation versus externally-claimed-as-closed distinction is binding. Fusion may use temporary locally closed internal support volumes as a numerical aid, but exported topology must preserve open boundaries and missing sectors when evidence is incomplete. These are explicitly different states and must never be conflated. [P+C][OC]
The staging is capability-defined, not model-locked. Any implementation satisfying the admission, coupled-trio fusion, fragment-preservation, confidence, topology-honesty, usefulness, and provenance contract is admissible. TSDF, MVS, dense point fusion, implicit fields, Poisson only within honesty bounds, NeRF / 3DGS-class appearance scaffolds, or capability-equivalent methods are all acceptable if the masking, weighting, and non-hallucination boundaries are obeyed. [P+G+C][PRD]
Observation admission and weighting policy
Admission is hybrid hard-gated plus soft-weighted. Three classes exist: hard-gated exclusion, soft-weighted admission, and rescue-only admission. This is stronger than pure gating or pure soft weighting because it preserves uncertainty without letting clearly invalid evidence contaminate dense geometry. [P+G+C][REG][SIAT]
Hard-gated exclusion is binding and narrow. An observation or pixel is excluded from dense contribution when REG registration_state is excluded; when SIAT exclusion_mask covers the relevant region; when occlusion_labels confirm an occluder; when pose_confidence_band is excluded; when patch_identity_disagreement is recorded upstream; or when photometric support collapses below the calibrated floor across the whole target matte on that observation. Exclusion is logged with reason and provenance. [P+G+C][REG][SIAT]
Soft-weighted admission is the default. Admitted observations contribute with weights derived from target_alpha_soft, target_core_mask, boundary_confidence_channel, pose_confidence_band, pose_covariance_digest, feature_support_regime, per_observation_alignment_health, SIAT ambiguity tags, FSCQI utility vectors, and reference_only_flag. Soft alpha is preserved through fusion. Silent binarization is forbidden. [P+G+C][SIAT][REG][FSCQI]
static_rigid_core is the highest-trust dense-support region. Pixels inside static_rigid_core on registered observations with strong pose confidence receive maximum fusion weight. The upstream rule remains binding: static_rigid_core is a texture-stability-defined support mask, not a claim of anatomical rigidity. [P+G+C][SIAT][REG]
Weakly_registered observations are admitted only as low-weight auxiliary support. Their contribution is subject to a surface-confidence ceiling, may support cautious low-trust local evidence accumulation, and is always marked in surface_fragment_provenance. Weakly_registered support alone may not produce strong or moderate target-surface claims without corroboration from stronger neighboring evidence. Sparse regions with only weakly_registered support become weak_surface_fragment or skeletal_support_only, not cosmetically filled surface. [P+G+C][REG]
Reference-only observations and components contribute zero target-surface vertices or surfels. They may inform missing-region diagnosis, appearance-support bookkeeping, and visibility reasoning, but they do not generate target-surface truth. [P+G+C][REG]
Retained context policy is bounded. target_adjacent_soft_context_band may be admitted at reduced weight for boundary disambiguation, ray-consistency checking, silhouette stabilization, and local normal regularization near the target edge, but it is reconstructed into a separately labeled context_geometry sub-layer. It is never fused into target-surface truth. Context may help the solve; it may not become target anatomy by leakage. [P+G+C][SIAT]
Rescue regime is bounded and logged. When target texture support is chronically weak on a REG component that is otherwise usable, DG may enter rescue mode for that component: retained-context assistance may increase only inside context_geometry; target-surface recovery thresholds tighten rather than relax; topology and usefulness may be downgraded; and rescue invocation is logged. Rescue may never elevate output above the ceiling set by REG component_status and may never relabel context_geometry as target surface. [P+G+C][SIAT][REG]
Admission is never based on operator-blameable grounds. Weakness widens uncertainty, lowers confidence, produces fragments, and restricts usefulness zones. It never back-propagates into stricter operator-facing capture discipline. [P+G+C][PRD][CUX]
Surface recovery and topology policy
Fragment preservation is the default posture. DG favors conservative surface fragments with honest open boundaries over forced closure. No automatic watertighting runs anywhere in the pipeline. No global completion, template-driven inpainting, or learned shape hallucination runs by default. Closure is asserted only where local support and consistency cross the calibrated threshold. [P+G+C][OC]
The internally-closed-versus-externally-claimed distinction is binding. Fusion may use temporary internally closed support volumes for computation, but exported topology must preserve open boundaries and missing sectors when evidence is incomplete. Internal numerical closure is not permission to export unsupported surface closure. [P+C][OC]
Open boundaries are a first-class output. Every open-boundary segment carries boundary_label ∈ {open_true_boundary, open_missing_evidence, open_ambiguous}; hole_class ∈ {not_observed, observed_but_not_reconstructable, occluded_or_confounded, registration_limited, topology_bridge_withheld}; and root_cause_tag ∈ {frame_edge_boundary, occlusion_boundary, signal_starvation_boundary} where applicable. Later layers need to know whether a gap is absent evidence, bad evidence, or intentionally withheld completion. [P+C][OC]
Topology classes remain structural rather than overloaded. Every fragment carries topology_confidence_class ∈ {closed_locally, open_with_honest_boundary, fragmented_open, skeletal_support_only}. Bridge dependence, merge weakness, unilateral-evidence caution, and workflow restrictions are expressed through fragment_relations, merge_confidence_matrix, weak_geometry_zones, surface_confidence_field, and usefulness zones rather than by exploding topology taxonomy itself. [P+G+C][OC]
Thin support, missing undersides, self-occluded sectors, and low-parallax zones are not hallucinated. They remain open, weak, or support-only unless corroborated by genuine multi-view evidence. DG may use bounded denoising or regularization, but may not extend surface beyond support merely to make outputs cleaner. [P+G+C][REG][OC]
Thickness-aware handling is required for thin anatomical structures such as ear flaps, skin folds, nostril rims, fingertip margins, and similarly thin surfaces. Front-and-back surfaces may not collapse into one plane and support volumes may not balloon unnaturally. Where thickness cannot be resolved honestly, the region is downgraded to weak_surface_fragment or skeletal_support_only with weakness_reason=poor_dense_support. [P+G+C][OC]
Disconnected pieces within and across REG components are admissible. A single component may yield multiple surface fragments if the evidence topology supports only local patches. Each fragment is preserved separately with its own confidence, topology class, usefulness distribution, and provenance. Aggressive pruning of every small fragment merely to make outputs cleaner is forbidden. [P+G+C][REG]
Bridges remain claims, not assumptions. A bridge hypothesis may exist as a weak_bridge relation or a cross_component_merge_report note, but it must not become fused surface unless both REG component_relations and dense corroboration support it strongly. [P+G+C][REG]
Watertightness is never an export target of this layer. Any later layer that wants watertight topology must own that transformation, own the risk, and maintain explicit lineage to the honest non-watertight DG output. [P+G+C][OC][PRD]
Smoothing is bounded and logged. Denoising, normal regularization, or confidence-weighted smoothing is admissible only if it does not close open boundaries, does not extend geometry beyond support, does not erase legitimate high-frequency anatomy merely to make the output prettier, and does not inflate confidence. [P+G+C][OC]
Representation strategy for downstream use
Later stages receive a multi-layered, co-registered representation, not a single monolithic surface. The framing is dual-role: a Structural Proxy for later deformation, parameterization, and placement workflows, plus an Appearance Scaffold for later photoreal appearance fusion or view synthesis. The coupled-trio dense representation plus conservative surface fragments jointly serve both roles so neither downstream workflow is forced to over-read the other’s substrate. [P+G+C][OC]
Structural Proxy layer: per-component conservative surface fragments, surface_confidence_field, topology_confidence_class, hole_and_open_boundary_map, fragment_relations, merge_confidence_matrix, usefulness zones, unusable_for_placement_zones, unusable_for_shape_preview_zones, and the separately labeled context_geometry sub-layer. These are the geometric surfaces later editing or parameterization-class layers may build on, subject to their own honesty constraints. [P+G+C][OC]
Appearance Scaffold layer: per-component fused oriented support points, volumetric or implicit support field, visibility and support-count accumulators, appearance_support_manifest, contributing observation lists, occlusion-consistent visibility masks, appearance_risk_flags, and reference-only appearance-support pointers. These let later appearance layers consume DG output without re-solving geometry from scratch. [P+G+C][OC]
Body-part pose frame continuity is binding. All representations are co-registered to REG body_part_pose_frame without DG-side modification. DG prepares cross-output spatial consistency by living in that frame; it does not strengthen the certainty of that frame. [P+G+C][REG][OC]
Scale-regime propagation is unchanged. scale_regime, scale_confidence_band, metric_trust_allowed, and measurement_validity_claim are inherited unchanged from REG. DG additionally emits measurement_use_prohibited as a derived explicit flag. Later layers may not infer stronger metric claims from the existence of a surface. [P+G+C][REG][OC][PRD]
DG explicitly distinguishes geometry evidence, conservative surface interpretation, workflow guidance, and honesty channels. The coupled-trio dense support is geometry evidence; partial surface fragments are the current conservative surface interpretation; usefulness zones are workflow guidance; boundary labels, hole classes, weakness reasons, and topology-confidence class are honesty outputs. Later prompts may not collapse these into one unqualified “mesh truth” object. [P+G+C][OC]
surface_usefulness_zones use the taxonomy {anchor_candidate, preview_candidate, appearance_only, reference_only, unusable}. anchor_candidate means locally strong enough for later surface-anchored placement to attempt parameterization; preview_candidate means locally strong enough for later bounded deformable preview to attempt; appearance_only means useful for appearance workflows but not for placement or preview; reference_only means support bookkeeping only; unusable means no downstream workflow should consume it. These are guidance classes, not final downstream guarantees. [P+G+C][OC]
Readiness booleans are derived session-level summary signals. geometry_ready_for_appearance ∈ {true, degraded, false}; geometry_ready_for_anchor_attempt ∈ {true, partial, false}; geometry_ready_for_preview_attempt ∈ {true, partial, false}. These are summaries only and do not replace zone-level semantics for binding downstream decisions. [P+G+C][OC]
Later prompts may not assume a single globally connected surface, watertight topology, uniform confidence, placement-ready surface everywhere, shape-preview-ready surface everywhere, absence of open boundaries, or metric-grade surface truth. [P+G+C][OC][PRD]
Weak geometry, incompleteness, and fragment policy
Partial but locally coherent surfaces are first-class successes. A fragment covering only part of the target with strong local support is emitted as usable_primary_surface or usable_secondary_surface with honest open boundaries and corresponding usefulness zoning. It is not withheld because full coverage was not achieved. [P+G+C][OC]
Missing sectors remain missing and explicit. DG emits hole_and_open_boundary_map entries for regions where evidence did not support surface recovery, with provenance back to FSCQI weak-region signals, SIAT isolation weakness, REG instability or low parallax, or local dense-support insufficiency. [P+G+C][FSCQI][SIAT][REG]
Weakly reconstructed zones are downgraded, not hidden. A region becomes appearance_only, reference_only, or unusable when it is visually useful or topologically informative but not trustworthy enough for later anchoring or shape preview. This is especially important under glare, hair, dressings, weak texture, ambiguity-tainted edges, or one-sided coverage. [P+G+C][OC][SIAT]
Disconnected fragments are preserved separately. No cosmetic joining is allowed merely because a single clean mesh would look better. [P+G+C][REG]
Weakness reasoning is mandatory and explicit. Every weak_geometry_zones entry carries weakness_reason ∈ {unobserved, ambiguous_isolation, pose_uncertainty, poor_dense_support, topology_withheld}. Lumping weakness classes together would make later QC and downstream refusal logic too coarse. [P+G+C][REG][SIAT][FSCQI]
The three-question fragment / downgrade / unusable rule is binding. First: does the fragment carry enough support for later appearance workflows from near-capture viewpoints? Second: does it carry enough support for stable surface anchoring later? Third: does it carry enough local continuity for bounded shape-preview deformation later? A fragment may be usable for the first and unusable for the second or third; DG must keep those distinctions explicit rather than flattening them. [P+G+C][OC]
Severe concern surfacing is bounded and two-tier. DG emits severe_geometry_concern when no usable_primary_component yields any anchor_candidate zone, when placement-relevant sectors are mostly weak or missing, when large fractions of the target resolve only to appearance_only or reference_only, or when patch attempts produce no dense support under admissible conditions. DG emits severe_topology_concern when standard fusion results in catastrophic mesh-soup behavior, when a previously dominant component shatters into many micro-fragments during extraction, when target_core_mask regions resolve entirely to unusable, or when pathologically incoherent geometry survives admissible stabilization. These are honest warnings to later QC, not session refusal and not a rewrite of FSCQI processability. [P+G+C][FSCQI][OC]
Patch interaction policy
Patch is repair, never redefinition, and never silent rewriting of earlier geometry. Patch integration occurs only under append-only geometry_version lineage. Pre-patch geometry remains pinned to the prior geometry_version; patch-integrated geometry appears as a new geometry_version under the same canonical session identity with explicit lineage to the prior geometry_version, the relevant pose_version, and the relevant isolation_version. In-place mutation is forbidden. [P+G+C][MOB][REG][SIAT]
Patch effects are classified as patch_effect_local_strengthened, patch_effect_bridge, patch_effect_local_new_fragment, patch_effect_no_geometry_gain, or patch_effect_refused. These classes are recorded per affected component and per patch report. [P+G+C][REG]
Local improvement is preferred. If a patch strengthens an existing weak region, DG updates only affected support and surface fragments where possible, records the delta, and explicitly notes unaffected regions. Session-global re-fusion is admissible only when REG itself emitted a compatible broader pose_version re-opt and all compatibility conditions hold. DG does not make session-global re-fusion decisions on its own support-sufficiency judgment. [P+G+C][REG]
Patch refusal is explicit, not silent. patch_geometry_refused carries reason codes such as patch_identity_disagreement_upstream, patch_pose_weak_below_admission, patch_isolation_conflict, patch_photometric_incompatible, patch_scale_regime_incompatible, or patch_bystander_geometry_culled. Refusal never mutates earlier geometry and never rewrites upstream state. [P+G+C][REG][SIAT]
Patch never crosses scale or camera-state incompatibility silently. Bridging across multi_scale_conflict or materially incompatible camera-state regimes is refused with reason code. [P+G+C][REG]
Every patch-affected region is tagged with patch_id, patch_reason, prior geometry_version pointer, prior-versus-posterior confidence classification, affected regions, support deltas, fragment changes, and usefulness-zone reclassification changes so later layers can read the chain without guesswork. [P+G+C][MOB][OC]

Performance and robustness posture
Inside the approved calm-capture envelope, DG silently tolerates weak texture, breathing and sway already absorbed upstream into pose uncertainty, mixed lighting, moderate glare and specular hotspots, partial occlusion, weakly_registered observations, multi-component sessions, M2 fallback sessions, patch additions, and inconsistent calibration quality already modeled by REG. These conditions widen uncertainty, reduce confidence, or produce open boundaries and usefulness restrictions; they do not cause routine collapse. [P+G+C][PRD][CUX][MOB][REG][SIAT]
DG respects SIAT glare semantics rather than re-adjudicating them. Where SIAT marks hard occlusion or glare-confounded boundary loss upstream, DG excludes accordingly; where SIAT preserves interior target support with specular uncertainty, DG reduces photometric weight and allows honest multi-view recovery only where soft alpha and boundary confidence permit it. DG does not independently decide to “punch a hole” for glare that SIAT has not already justified upstream. [P+G+C][SIAT]
DG raises concern when dense support collapses across most of the target despite PROCESS_CLEAN and usable REG geometry, when chronic photometric inconsistency survives weighting, when no REG component yields any usable surface fragment, when patch attempts produce no dense gain under admissible conditions, or when pathologically incoherent geometry survives admissible stabilization. These are honest flags, not session refusal. [P+G+C][FSCQI][REG]
Compute posture is explicit. Primary-tier observations participate first. Extended-tier observations are admitted lazily where rescue or ambiguity repair requires them. Under compute pressure the degradation order is: defer extended-tier admission; coarsen per-observation dense-support resolution; coarsen component fusion resolution; reduce already-weak-zone refinement; skip bounded smoothing; preserve primary fragment extraction, confidence fields, boundary maps, usefulness zones, severe-concern flags, and provenance last. DG must fit within its share of the self-hosted minutes-not-hours budget and must not become the hidden bottleneck. [P+G+C][PRD][FSCQI]
What must never happen: silent conversion of weak support into strong confidence; silent closure of open boundaries; silent hole-filling; silent global merging across REG components; silent patch-driven rewrite of prior geometry_version; silent rescue-regime elevation above REG ceilings; silent leakage of retained context into target surface; silent suppression of severe concerns; or backward leakage of stricter capture requirements onto the operator. [P+G+C][PRD][OC][CUX][REG]
Acceptance criteria
DG-AC-1 — Standard-session target recovery. On FSCQI PROCESS_CLEAN, REG usable_primary_component sessions within the calm-capture envelope, DG reliably produces one dominant surface fragment covering the large majority of the REG primary component’s admitted target-core support area, with strong or moderate confidence over the large majority of that recovered area. [P+G][PRD][FSCQI][REG]
DG-AC-2 — Graceful partial recovery. On PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS sessions, weak-component sessions, multi-component sessions, or sessions with weakly_registered observations, DG still produces at least one usable fragment or support-only output in the large majority of applicable cases, with honest open boundaries, confidence bands, and usefulness-zone labeling rather than cosmetic completion or silent collapse. [P+G][FSCQI][REG][OC]
DG-AC-3 — False-completion suppression. In regions where no camera visual ray penetrated, the exported surface exhibits a literal open boundary or hole in 100% of applicable cases. False watertightness rate is 0%. [P+G+C][OC]
DG-AC-4 — Fragment honesty. Disconnected REG components produce disconnected fragments unless both REG relation support and dense corroboration justify strong_bridge. [P+G+C][REG]
DG-AC-5 — Boundary labeling completeness. Every emitted open-boundary segment carries boundary_label and hole_class in 100% of cases, with root_cause_tag present wherever applicable. [P+C][OC]
DG-AC-6 — Weak-component discipline. REG weak_component inputs never produce usable_primary_surface outputs. They produce at most usable_secondary_surface, weak_surface_fragment, or skeletal_support_only with corresponding confidence and usefulness ceilings. [P+G+C][REG]

The relationship is permanently 1:1: one accepted session yields one asset root, and that asset root never rebinds to another session. They are semantically distinct — one names capture origin, the other names the persisted backend asset family — because the brief explicitly required defining that relationship. Implementation may alias the two values to the same underlying identifier; the contract keeps the distinction explicit so downstream consumers have clean semantics and later lifecycle operations can operate over the asset-family concept. [P][G][C][MOB][OC]
Under the asset root, OQSP maintains an append-only lineage DAG with seven mandatory stage axes plus one publish axis: [P][G][C][FSCQI][SIAT][REG][DG][EDSIM]
fscqi_bundle_version — FSCQI curated-observation package lineage. Patch integration explicitly re-curates the session and alters the admitted observation package; the foundational input change needs a discrete node so derivation_context_id resolution remains non-fragile.
isolation_version — SIAT bundle lineage.
pose_version — REG bundle lineage, including poses, calibration state, and scale regime.
geometry_version — DG bundle lineage, including structural-proxy and appearance-scaffold sub-layers.
view_version — photoreal view-synthesis branch lineage, realizing R-OUT-1. Explicit because R-OUT-1 is a binding output-capability class and may evolve independently of structural geometry.
edit_sim_version — EDSIM bundle lineage.
qc_version — OQSP-synthesized QC/flag bundle lineage.
publish_version — immutable publish-manifest lineage, minted as a frozen bundle view over a coherent tuple of underlying stage versions. [P][G][C][OC]
Every version node carries: axis version number, stage_contract_version, pipeline_fingerprint, exact parent pointers, derivation_context_id, compat_class, artifact_manifest_hash, and validity state; publish nodes additionally carry lineage_status. [P][G][C]
derivation_context_id is a cryptographic hash of the stage contract version, the pipeline fingerprint, the set of parent version IDs, any explicit external references, and the material execution parameters that affect output behavior. It is the reuse-vs-re-derivation primitive: unchanged context means retry/resume continues the same version; changed context mandates a new version. [P][G][C][OC]
compat_class ∈ {compatible_rebuild, new_derivation_context, new_stage_contract}. Per-stage validity states are still_valid, stale_requires_review, and retired. Per-publish lineage states are current, superseded_compatible, superseded_incompatible, and retired. Older descendants remain retrievable. Published bundles never silently heal, and EDSIM persistent states only auto-rebind when they are identical within the declared drift budget; otherwise they become stale or retired. [P][G][C][EDSIM]
Publishing is a deterministic manifest view over immutable stage lineage, not a new scientific content layer. Any meaning-changing transformation belongs upstream or in qc_version; publish_version freezes and exposes a lineage tuple. [P][G][C][OC]
4. Workflow orchestration strategy
OQSP uses a hybrid durable state machine over a branching stage DAG. The lifecycle state machine owns lifecycle truth:
INGEST_PENDING → INGEST_VERIFIED → CURATED → ISOLATED → REGISTERED → GEOMETRY_READY → {VIEW_READY, EDIT_SIM_READY} → QC_READY → PUBLISHED
with FAILED_RESUMABLE, FAILED_UNRECOVERABLE, SKIPPED_COMPATIBLE_REUSE, and SUPERSEDED as additional stage/publish outcomes. [P][G][C]
The DAG branches at DG:
ingest → FSCQI → SIAT → REG → DG → {view-synthesis, EDSIM} → QC synthesis → publish
This branching is chosen because EDSIM operates on the geometric substrate before final appearance integration and may coexist with compatible appearance-side context in parallel. A forced serial DG→view→EDSIM order over-constrains that approved upstream posture. [P][C][EDSIM][DG]
Stage transitions occur only upon durable write and validation of the predecessor’s mandatory contract artifacts, fingerprint signing, hash verification, and parent-pointer resolution. A stage never starts on partial or unsigned input. [P][G][C]
Each stage is idempotent under derivation_context_id. Retries that find the same context reuse the same version and resume from the last durable sub-checkpoint where supported. REG and DG support sub-checkpointing; SIAT, view-synthesis, and EDSIM are typically whole-stage reruns on fresh input. Tier 3 scratch is disposable; Tier 1 and Tier 2 are authoritative. [P][G][C][MOB]
Partial failures are stage-local by default. FSCQI failure does not corrupt ingest; SIAT failure does not corrupt FSCQI; DG failure does not invalidate REG. Later stages reuse earlier outputs whenever parent version IDs and derivation_context_id remain unchanged. [P][G][C]
FAILED_RESUMABLE covers worker restart, transient hardware pressure, mitigable OOM, network hiccup, IO jitter, temporary Tier 3 loss, and recoverable storage pressure. FAILED_UNRECOVERABLE covers corrupt input, schema violation, impossible parentage, hash mismatch, unparseable output, pipeline-fingerprint mismatch, and catastrophic unmitigable OOM. A failed-unrecoverable derivation context strictly halts its publish path; no new publish may be minted from that broken branch. Previously valid earlier publishes remain valid historical states. [P][G][C]
Patch-triggered recomputation is local-first and lineage-driven. A patch creates a new fscqi_bundle_version, then the orchestrator walks forward from the earliest affected stage. Each stage declares whether the patch effect is local_only, bridge, global_reopt, or refused, and OQSP records and propagates that without overriding it. [P][G][C][SIAT][REG][DG][EDSIM]
Manual review is not on the critical path. OQSP emits machine-readable review-required states, but does not own the review workflow. Review rendering and adjudication belong entirely to downstream UI layers. [P][G][C][CUX][EDSIM]
5. Stage contract and handoff policy
Major stage handoffs happen only through durable, content-addressed, explicitly typed manifests. There is no trusted in-memory handoff across stage boundaries. Each manifest points to immutable artifacts by hash and declares the exact upstream versions it consumed. [P][G][C]
Valid stage completion requires:
all mandatory outputs present,
schema validation against stage_contract_version,
all artifact hashes matching,
parent pointers resolved,
append-only storage discipline preserved,
signature by the declared pipeline_fingerprint. [P][G][C]
A stage may complete with weak, fragmented, guarded, appearance-only, or refusal-bearing content as long as the content is valid under its own upstream contract. That includes FSCQI PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS, REG weakly registered components, DG skeletal_support_only or fragmented open surfaces, degraded view outputs, and EDSIM placement_only, preview_only, appearance_only, or no_edit_authority. Those are valid completions, not orchestration failures. [P][G][C][FSCQI][REG][DG][EDSIM]
Internal failure requiring rerun exists only for contract or execution failure. Weakness, guardedness, and refusal are never orchestration failures. [P][G][C]
Pass-through-first rule:
FSCQI hands off curated tiers, processability verdict, weak-region register, patch package, provenance, and unchanged mobile provenance.
SIAT adds masks, ambiguity, retained context, and isolation weak regions.
REG adds body-part pose frame, registration graph, scale regime, instability, and measurement posture.
DG adds fragments, hole/open-boundary semantics, topology/usefulness outputs, structural proxy, and appearance scaffold.
View synthesis adds the photoreal viewing realization and appearance-risk outputs.
EDSIM adds placement/preview authority maps, refusal zones, appearance-only routes, stale/rebind records, and edit readiness.
QC synthesis consumes and augments all of the above. [P][G][C]
Every stage handoff records both stage_contract_version and pipeline_fingerprint. Contract bumps change meaning or required fields; fingerprint changes capture internal behavioral changes. Both participate in derivation_context_id. Silent semantic or tuning migration is forbidden. [P][G][C]
6. Storage architecture
OQSP uses three strict storage tiers. [P][G][C]
Tier 1 — Immutable content store. Canonical for all content: raw media, session manifests, stage artifacts, provenance files, view artifacts, QC artifacts, publish manifests, regeneration manifests. Every object is SHA-256 content-addressed and append-only. Mutation in place is a contract violation. [P][G][C]
Tier 2 — Relational lineage/control store. Canonical for relationships: asset roots, version axes, parent/child edges, derivation contexts, compatibility classes, stale/rebind records, publish indices, workflow state, retention state, audit indices, and query paths. [P][G][C]
Tier 3 — Transient working cache. Worker scratch only. Never authoritative. Cache loss is wasted compute, not data loss. [P][G][C]
Within each asset root, content is organized into raw/, stage/, qc/, and publish/ families. Each leaf bundle has a manifest with hashes, sizes, schema version, fingerprint, derivation context, parent version tuple, and retention class. [P][C]
Tier 1 and Tier 2 ordering may not rely solely on wall-clock timestamps. Every append-only write and lineage/audit event carries a monotonic, tamper-evident sequencing mechanism so concurrent patch and supersession events resolve deterministically. Wall-clock timestamps remain operational metadata only. [P][C]
Retention is lineage-aware and deployment-configurable. Raw media and provenance survive at least as long as any active publish or regeneration guarantee depends on them. Intermediate artifacts may move to colder storage but are not deleted while referenced by live publishes, stale/rebind states, or audit obligations. Purge is explicit, logged, tombstoned, and cascade-safe; it may never strand a published bundle claiming reproducibility while required parents are gone. [P][G][C][OC]
Published views reference immutable underlying artifacts by hash. Publishing does not silently duplicate and reinterpret content. If required underlying artifacts are retired, dependent publishes are explicitly retired too. [P][G][C]
7. QC / metadata / flagged-region synthesis strategy
QC synthesis is the honesty-preservation core of OQSP. Per-layer honesty outputs remain available exactly as emitted; the fused QC output augments them with an asset-level operational view and never replaces them. [P][G][C]
Fusion follows two named rules:
Union operation: weakness accumulates across layers; strength in one layer never cancels weakness in another.
Most conservative signal dictates authoritative bounding: where layers disagree, the strictest applicable signal sets the final authority ceiling for the affected capability. [P][G][C]
The mandatory QC artifact set is eight outputs:
asset_qc_manifest.json
flagged_region_inventory.jsonl
capability_readiness_map.json
operation_conflict_register.json
measurement_posture.json
severe_concern_aggregation.json
lineage_honesty_summary.json
qc_decision_log.jsonl [P][G][C]
Each entry in flagged_region_inventory.jsonl carries:
region_id
operation_scope ∈ {view, placement, preview, measurement, global}
spatial_binding_type ∈ {atlas_guidance, observation_mask, fragment_surface, boundary_segment, anchor_domain, preview_region, asset_global}
binding_payload
effective_status
source_weakness_trace
source_layers
reason_chain
fused_severity_band ∈ {informational, cautionary, guarded_use_only, unsuitable}
affected_capabilities
root_cause_synthesis
downstream_authority_effect
confidence_envelope
upstream_version_pointers
provenance_chain. [P][G][C]
effective_status is cause-aware, not generic. Example values include view_degraded_due_to_pose_uncertainty, placement_guarded_due_to_open_ambiguous_boundary, preview_appearance_only_due_to_poor_dense_support, and measurement_prohibited_due_to_relative_only_scale. [P][G][C]
What stays per-layer: observation-level and fragment-level evidence, stage-native maps, and named provenance. What becomes asset-level: fused flagged regions, capability readiness, operation conflicts, measurement posture, severe concern aggregation, lineage honesty summary, and publishability class. [P][G][C]
The descriptive readiness summary is mandatory but non-gating:
asset_appearance_readiness ∈ {ready, degraded, unavailable}
asset_placement_readiness ∈ {region_wide, partial, none}
asset_preview_readiness ∈ {region_wide, partial, none}
asset_measurement_posture inherited unchanged. [P][G][C]
A single scalar asset confidence score is forbidden. A whole-asset traffic-light gate is forbidden. Region-level maps always win. [P][G][C]
qc_version is append-only. Any change to fusion logic, schema, or decision policy mints a new QC version. [P][G][C]
8. Publishing policy
Publishing means minting an immutable publish_version manifest that references one coherent lineage tuple and exposes the asset to downstream layers under explicit readiness and honesty semantics. Publishing is not permission to hide uncertainty. [P][G][C]
The minimum external publish floor is:
ingest integrity verified,
FSCQI processable or validly recovered via patch,
valid REG bundle with body-part pose frame,
valid DG bundle,
valid view-capable route fulfilling the viewing contract,
valid EDSIM bundle,
completed QC bundle,
all hashes and parent pointers resolved,
no failed-unrecoverable branch contributes to the publish tuple. [P][G][C][OC]
A view-capable route may be a photoreal view representation or an honest appearance-only route for viewing. A purely structural or edit-capable internal state without an honest viewing route remains valid lineage but does not externally publish. [P][G][C][OC]
Publishability is layered through seven classes:
publishable_full
publishable_view_and_placement
publishable_view_and_preview
publishable_view_only
publishable_appearance_only_with_refusals
publishable_partial
unpublishable [P][G][C]
The publishability class is a navigational headline only. Per-region maps remain the sole authoritative basis for actual operations. [P][G][C]
Placement authority remains three-class. Preview authority remains four-class. Appearance-only preview routes remain non-persistent. Stale/rebind status remains explicit. [P][G][C][EDSIM]
Measurement posture is always top-level and machine-readable:
scale_regime, scale_confidence_band, metric_trust_allowed, measurement_validity_claim, and measurement_use_prohibited. [P][G][C][REG][DG]
Patch-updated descendants never overwrite prior published states. A new publish_version becomes active. Earlier publishes remain immutable historical states with explicit supersession status. [P][G][C]
9. Re-run, invalidation, and patch-update policy
A lineage-changing event is any new stage version, any QC-version increment, any publish-version increment, any stage-contract-version bump, any fingerprint change that changes derivation context, or any mobile patch that causes one of those outcomes. Such events never rewrite history. [P][G][C]
Invalidation is dependency-scoped and local-first. A patch creates a new fscqi_bundle_version, then downstream invalidation follows declared dependency edges, not blanket full reruns. [P][G][C]
Stage-specific scopes:
new fscqi_bundle_version: downstream reevaluates only if admitted observations or weak-region semantics materially changed
new isolation_version: REG, DG, view, EDSIM, QC, and publish reevaluate
new pose_version: DG, view, EDSIM, QC, and publish reevaluate; scope depends on REG’s declared patch effect
new geometry_version: view, EDSIM, QC, and publish reevaluate
new view_version: QC and publish reevaluate
new edit_sim_version: QC and publish reevaluate
new qc_version: publish reevaluates
new contract version: affected axis and all downstream affected axes mint fresh descendants. [P][G][C]
A downstream state may remain still_valid only when the stage explicitly declares compatible reuse for the new parent tuple. Otherwise it becomes stale_requires_review or retired. Silent adjusted remapping is forbidden. [P][G][C][EDSIM]
Previously published bundles remain valid but superseded. They stay retrievable and content-verified. A new publish is minted for the new lineage tuple. [P][G][C]

11. Privacy, security, and access-control posture
All raw capture media, stage artifacts, QC outputs, publish manifests, and lineage metadata are encrypted at rest. All transport is encrypted in transit. Raw-capture and derived-output storage are separated so ordinary downstream consumption never implies raw-media access. [P][G][C][PRD]
Access control is per asset root. Minimum roles are operator, reviewer, admin, and service, with service credentials scoped to the specific asset and stage invocation. OQSP stores only the minimum authorization metadata needed to enforce access. [P][G][C]
Auditability is mandatory. Every read, write, publish, export, purge, stale/rebind event, access denial, and supersession event is recorded in an append-only, tamper-evident audit log with actor, asset root, artifact/bundle reference, action, wall-clock timestamp, and monotonic ordering reference. [P][G][C]
Telemetry is strictly limited to event-count and state-transition form: timings, retries, compute pressure, invalidation counts, publishability distributions, and similar operational signals. It may not include raw imagery, thumbnails, masks, subject identifiers, clinic identity, location, or any content-bearing payload. Telemetry is opt-in per deployment and never required for core processing. [P][G][C][MOB]
True self-hosting is binding: no required runtime dependency on external reconstruction APIs, license servers, hosted QC services, or analytics services. [P][G][C][PRD]
12. Observability, diagnostics, and operator support
OQSP must log stage transitions, queue wait time, execution time, retries, resumptions, validation results, hash mismatches, missing artifacts, schema violations, publish validation results, compatible-reuse decisions, patch-triggered invalidations, stale/rebind outcomes, and publish-to-lineage drift. Every event keys off asset root, session identity, version, workflow run ID, and monotonic sequence reference. [P][G][C]
Three mandatory dashboards:
Capture-to-backend honesty dashboard
Lineage health dashboard
Publish honesty dashboard. [P][G][C]
Four named sensitivity regression tripwires:
FSCQI retry-rate or below-floor inflation
EDSIM refusal-rate inflation
publishable-partial rate creep without matching quality shift
patch-triggered rerun excess. [P][G][C]
Authorized investigators must be able to trace directly to upstream decision logs and provenance artifacts by hash reference, not only the fused summary. Diagnosis support remains backend-contract-only: OQSP emits machine-readable reasons and lineage traces but does not prescribe the UI workflow around them. [P][G][C]

14. Performance and robustness posture
OQSP must tolerate partial captures, PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS sessions, valid patch descendants, weak priors, weakly registered components, fragmented/open geometry, partial edit authority, long lineage chains, worker restarts, interrupted jobs, and normal self-hosted compute/storage pressure. Its normal response is honest narrowing or partial publication, not collapse. [P][G][C]
Minutes-not-hours operation comes from local-first invalidation, compatible reuse, immutable parent caching, and stage-specific degradation rules that preserve core outputs and honesty channels last. Compute pressure may widen uncertainty or delay secondary packaging, but it may not silently drop honesty channels or mutate artifacts into cosmetically cleaner forms. [P][G][C]
Escalation is reserved for hard operational truths: cryptographic integrity failure, impossible parentage, hash mismatch, repeated unrecoverable drift, hard storage exhaustion, or publish-manifest drift from underlying lineage. None of these may back-propagate as stricter capture demands on the operator. [P][G][C]
15. Acceptance criteria
AC-1 Identity correctness: one accepted session yields exactly one asset root, with no duplicate roots or silent re-rooting. [P][G][C]
AC-2 Identity semantics: capture-origin identity and asset-family identity remain semantically distinct but permanently 1:1 bound. [P][G][C]
AC-3 Append-only discipline: zero in-place mutation of stage versions, QC versions, or publish manifests. [P][G][C]
AC-4 Durable execution: every integrity-verified session reaches a terminal backend state with durable lineage. [P][G][C]
AC-5 Hash integrity: every content reference resolves and verifies by hash; mismatches halt the consuming path. [P][G][C]
AC-6 Stage handoff determinism: every handoff verifies schema, hashes, parent pointers, and fingerprint before the next stage starts. [P][G][C]
AC-7 FSCQI lineage correctness: patch re-curation produces an append-only fscqi_bundle_version descendant when the curated package materially changes. [P][G]
AC-8 View lineage correctness: view realization changes that materially change photoreal output mint a new view_version rather than silently hiding inside another axis. [P][OC][C]
AC-9 Honest partial publishability: partial-but-valid upstream outputs can flow to the correct publishability class without a second hidden processability gate. [P][G][C]
AC-10 External publish floor: externally published assets always satisfy the honest view-capable-route requirement. [P][G][C][OC]
AC-11 QC union fidelity: every upstream weakness/refusal/ambiguity/stale signal is preserved directly or through source_weakness_trace. [P][G][C]
AC-12 Conservative bounding correctness: where layers disagree, final authoritative bounding matches the strictest applicable upstream signal. [P][G][C]
AC-13 Region-level authority survives publishing: publish manifests always retain authority/refusal/stale/appearance-only semantics. [P][G][C]
AC-14 Measurement honesty preservation: scale and measurement posture propagate unchanged into publish manifests. [P][G][C]
AC-15 Patch-aware invalidation: every lineage-changing patch produces explicit descendants and explicit downstream validity states. [P][G][C]
AC-16 No silent rebind: persistent edit states are only auto-rebound when explicitly allowed within declared drift budget; otherwise they become stale or retired. [P][C][EDSIM]
AC-17 Prior publish retrievability: non-retired prior publishes remain retrievable and content-verified after later descendants exist. [P][C]
AC-18 Privacy and audit compliance: raw captures remain encrypted at rest; telemetry remains content-free; audit events are always logged with tamper-evident ordering. [P][G][C]
AC-19 Bundle completeness: every published asset contains all mandatory sub-bundles and all mandatory honesty channels. [P][G][C]
AC-20 Publish-to-lineage consistency: a publish manifest never points at mutable, missing, or invalid underlying artifacts. [P][G][C]
16. Failure conditions
Published bundles flatten away weak zones, refusal maps, stale states, or source weakness traces. [P][G][C]
Patch descendants overwrite earlier states or silently relocate persistent edits. [P][G][C]
QC synthesis hides disagreement by pretending stronger confidence than evidence supports. [P][G][C]
Storage loses raw provenance, named provenance artifacts, or lineage edges still referenced by live publishes. [P][G][C]
Publishability is treated as binary rather than layered. [P][G][C]
Stale or superseded states are served as current without explicit lineage status. [P][G][C]
Measurement prohibition or weak scale posture is lost downstream. [P][G][C]
Ordinary PROCESS_WITH_FLAGS sessions become backend dead ends. [P][G][C]
A second asset root is minted accidentally for a patch descendant. [P][G][C]
A publish manifest points at mutable or non-content-addressed content. [P][G][C]
Telemetry or logs contain content-bearing or re-identifying subject data. [P][G][C]
Required honesty channels are dropped under compute or storage pressure. [P][G][C]
Structural proxy and appearance scaffold are silently conflated into one payload. [P][G][C][DG]
Union-fusion is violated and strength in one layer cancels weakness in another. [P][G][C]
A failed-unrecoverable derivation branch is externally published. [P][G][C]
Manual review becomes a blocking orchestration gate for ordinary sessions. [P][G][C]
Stage reuse occurs where a new derivation context should have minted a fresh version. [P][G][C]
Retention purge silently breaks regeneration guarantees for live publishes. [P][G][C][OC]
Monotonic ordering is missing, making supersession or patch resolution ambiguous. [P][G][C]
An external publish is minted without an honest view-capable route. [P][G][C][OC]
17. Critical open risks
R-1 Lineage complexity at scale: deep DAGs may strain metadata performance and human debuggability. [P][G][C]
R-2 QC noise versus usefulness: preserving every cause chain may overwhelm downstream consumers if they misuse summaries or ignore region maps. [P][G][C]
R-3 Publishability edge cases: unusual mixed-region assets may stress the chosen class taxonomy. [P][G][C]
R-4 Patch invalidation scope accuracy: under-recompute risks stale outputs; over-recompute wastes time and compute. [P][G][C]
R-5 Storage pressure from large artifact families: raw retention plus many descendants may stress modest self-hosted deployments. [P][G][C]
R-6 Reproducibility under self-hosted variation: practical equivalence across different hardware/software stacks needs validation. [P][G][C]
R-7 Downstream honesty preservation: downstream consumers may still flatten authority/refusal/stale signals. [P][G][C]
R-8 Compatibility-reuse correctness: reuse logic must remain semantically safe as contracts and fingerprints evolve. [P][G][C]
R-9 QC-evolution comparability: comparing publishes across QC-version evolution may be harder than expected. [P][G][C]
R-10 Retention versus regeneration guarantees: purge policy can silently break R-OUT-4 if cascade logic is implemented poorly. [P][G][C][OC]
R-11 FSCQI lineage granularity: the operational burden of always maintaining fscqi_bundle_version as an explicit first-class axis needs validation against real patch behavior and storage/query cost. [P][G]
R-12 View-lineage independence: the operational cost and benefit of keeping view_version fully independent from geometry lineage needs validation under real deployment evolution. [P][C][OC]
18. Final binding orchestration/QC/storage/publishing contract
OQSP is the final backend execution and asset-formation layer. It accepts one integrity-verified mobile session, binds it permanently 1:1 to one persisted asset-family root, executes the approved pipeline under a durable hybrid DAG/state-machine model, stores all raw and derived artifacts append-only under content addressing, synthesizes final machine-readable QC and flagged-region outputs without flattening upstream nuance, and publishes immutable manifest views over coherent lineage tuples. [P][G][C]
It is bound to preserve, not beautify. It may not invent stronger geometry, stronger edit authority, stronger scale validity, stronger completeness, or stronger confidence than upstream contracts emitted. It may not silently mutate prior versions, silently rebind persistent edits, silently purge required provenance, silently upgrade measurement posture, or publish from integrity-failed branches. Partial-but-workflow-alive assets are valid success states and must publish where the honest publish floor is met. [P][G][C]
One canonical capture-origin identity binds 1:1 to one canonical asset-family identity for life. Lineage is append-only. fscqi_bundle_version, view_version, qc_version, and publish_version are explicit descendant axes. Publishing is a deterministic composite manifest over immutable stage outputs and QC synthesis, not a hidden new scientific content layer. [P][G][C][OC]
Every published asset must carry machine-readable flagged regions, authority/refusal/stale semantics, scale and measurement posture, lineage summary, and regeneration pointers. Region-level authority always wins over descriptive summaries and publishability-class headlines. [P][G][C]
The governing principle is binding: publishing is not permission to hide uncertainty. OQSP exists to turn upstream honest subsystem outputs into a durable, reproducible, publishable asset family with append-only lineage, preserved refusal/weakness semantics, machine-readable QC, and partial-but-workflow-alive publication where justified — not into a cosmetically simplified artifact that conceals uncertainty. [P][G][C]
